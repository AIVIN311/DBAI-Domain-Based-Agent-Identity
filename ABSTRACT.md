# DBAI Abstract

The rapid proliferation of AI agents and automated digital actors has outpaced the development
of persistent and interoperable identity infrastructure. Current identity models are often
platform-bound, wallet-fragmented, or dependent on runtime-specific credentials, limiting
cross-platform discoverability and long-term actor persistence.

DBAI (Domain-Based Agent Identity) proposes a minimal identity discovery convention that
leverages domain namespaces as durable anchor points for actor identification. By publishing
cryptographically verifiable agent manifests under domain control, DBAI enables decentralized
resolvers to discover and validate actor identities without reliance on centralized registries
or platform-specific accounts.

The design intentionally separates identity persistence from semantic, capability, and
reputation layers, allowing these concerns to evolve independently through extensions and
emergent indexing ecosystems. Domain-based namespace scoping further provides decentralized
semantic governance while maintaining global uniqueness.

Rather than introducing a new identity authority, DBAI positions itself as a glue layer between
naming systems, decentralized identifiers, wallet-based capabilities, and runtime-specific
credentials. This approach aims to support the emergence of an agent-centric web in which
digital actors can be persistently referenced, discovered, and composed across heterogeneous
environments.

DBAI’s long-term vision is to contribute a minimal, neutral identity substrate that helps AI
agents transition from ephemeral processes to persistent participants within open network
infrastructures.
